Meet Day Checklist

Everything you need packed, planned, and ready.

8 min read Updated Dec 2024

Competition day is not the time to realize you forgot your belt. This checklist covers everything—pack it the night before, check it twice, and focus on lifting.

The Golden Rule

Nothing new on meet day. Don't wear new shoes, try new foods, or test new equipment. Everything should be tested and familiar. Meet day is about execution, not experimentation.

Equipment Checklist

Required Equipment

  • ☐ Singlet (federation-approved)
  • ☐ T-shirt (for under singlet, no logos on some feds)
  • ☐ Belt (if using)
  • ☐ Squat shoes or lifting shoes
  • ☐ Deadlift shoes/slippers (if different from squat)
  • ☐ Knee sleeves
  • ☐ Wrist wraps
  • ☐ Long socks (for deadlift - required in most feds)
  • ☐ Underwear (briefs, not boxers - check rules)

Backup Equipment

  • ☐ Backup singlet (if you have one)
  • ☐ Extra t-shirt
  • ☐ Extra socks
  • ☐ Extra underwear

Training Aids

  • ☐ Chalk (block and/or liquid)
  • ☐ Ammonia/smelling salts (if you use them)
  • ☐ Resistance bands (for warm-up)
  • ☐ Foam roller (optional)
  • ☐ Lacrosse ball (optional)

Nutrition & Hydration

Food to Pack

  • ☐ Familiar, easily digestible foods
  • ☐ Simple carbs (rice cakes, white bread, fruit)
  • ☐ Protein source (deli meat, protein shake)
  • ☐ Salty snacks (if rehydrating after cut)
  • ☐ Candy/quick sugar (for between attempts if needed)
  • ☐ Caffeine source (pre-workout, coffee, caffeine pills)

Hydration

  • ☐ Water bottles (multiple)
  • ☐ Electrolyte drinks (Pedialyte, Liquid IV, etc.)
  • ☐ Sports drink
  • ☐ Cooler (if needed)

Documents & Admin

Required Documents

  • ☐ Federation membership card
  • ☐ Photo ID (driver's license, passport)
  • ☐ Meet registration confirmation
  • ☐ Attempt card (sometimes provided at meet)

Helpful to Have

  • ☐ Attempt selection written down
  • ☐ Warm-up plan written down
  • ☐ Phone charger
  • ☐ Cash (for vendors, food, parking)
  • ☐ Meet schedule/flight times

Comfort Items

Between Lifts

  • ☐ Flip flops/sandals (for between lifts)
  • ☐ Warm clothes (hoodie, sweatpants)
  • ☐ Blanket or sleeping bag (for long waits)
  • ☐ Camp chair (not all venues have seating)
  • ☐ Headphones
  • ☐ Phone with music/podcasts
  • ☐ Book or entertainment

Personal Care

  • ☐ Deodorant
  • ☐ Hair ties (if needed)
  • ☐ Baby powder (for singlet/thighs)
  • ☐ Ibuprofen/pain reliever
  • ☐ Any medications you take
  • ☐ Toilet paper (venue bathrooms sometimes run out)

Meet Day Timeline

Night Before

  • Pack everything using this checklist
  • Lay out clothes for weigh-in
  • Set multiple alarms
  • Get to bed early (you won't sleep great—that's normal)
  • Review attempt selection one more time

Morning Of

  • Wake up with plenty of time
  • Weigh yourself at home if possible
  • Light meal if eating before weigh-in
  • Arrive at venue 30+ minutes before weigh-in opens

Weigh-In

  • Check in, show ID and membership
  • Weigh in (singlet check usually happens here too)
  • Submit opening attempts
  • Get your rack heights
  • Find out your flight and lifting order

After Weigh-In

  • Begin rehydrating immediately
  • Eat your planned foods
  • Find a spot to set up your gear
  • Calculate when to start warming up
  • Relax—conserve energy for lifting

Warm-Up Timing

  • Start warming up ~30-45 minutes before your flight starts
  • Watch the flight before you to gauge timing
  • Take your last warm-up 2-3 attempts before you lift
  • Don't rush—better to be slightly early than late

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Forgetting ID or membership card: Some meets won't let you lift without them
  • Not checking equipment rules: Wrong singlet, wrong sleeve length = no lift
  • Overeating after weigh-in: You need fuel, not a feast
  • Warming up too early/late: Time it so you're ready when called
  • Forgetting long socks: Required for deadlifts in most federations
  • No backup plan: Belt breaks, shoes forgotten—have contingencies

Handler/Coach Responsibilities

If you have someone helping you:

  • Track attempt timing and call you when it's time
  • Load warm-up weights
  • Submit attempt changes
  • Keep you hydrated and fed
  • Handle logistics so you can focus on lifting
  • Know your planned attempts and when to adjust
First Meet Tip

If it's your first meet, pack more than you think you need. You'll learn what you actually use and can trim down for future meets. Better to have it and not need it.
