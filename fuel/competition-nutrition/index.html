Competition Day Nutrition

What to eat before, during, and after your meet.

10 min read Updated Dec 2024

Meet day nutrition is about maintaining energy without stomach distress. You're not trying to optimize—you're trying not to mess up something that's already dialed in from months of training.

The Cardinal Rule

Nothing new on meet day. Every food you eat should be something you've eaten before training without issues. This is not the time to try that new protein bar or energy drink.

The Day Before

Meet day nutrition starts the day before:

  • Eat normally—don't overeat trying to "carb load"
  • Stay hydrated (unless cutting weight)
  • Avoid anything that might cause GI distress
  • Skip alcohol completely
  • Get to bed at a reasonable hour

Morning of the Meet

If You're NOT Cutting Weight

Pre-Meet Breakfast

Eat 2-3 hours before you expect to lift your first squat. Choose familiar, easily digestible foods.

  • Moderate protein (eggs, Greek yogurt)
  • Moderate carbs (toast, oatmeal, banana)
  • Low fiber (to avoid GI issues)
  • Low fat (digests faster)
  • Nothing too large—you want energy, not fullness

If You ARE Cutting Weight

Pre-Weigh-In

  • Minimal food and water the morning of weigh-in
  • Small sips of water if needed
  • Save eating for after you make weight

After Weigh-In

This is critical, especially if you've cut weight. You have limited time to rehydrate and refuel before lifting.

Immediate (0-30 minutes post weigh-in)

  • Start drinking electrolytes immediately (Pedialyte, Liquid IV)
  • Sip continuously—don't chug
  • Begin eating carbs (rice cakes, white bread, fruit)
  • Small amounts of sodium to help retain fluid

30-90 Minutes Post Weigh-In

  • Continue hydrating
  • Eat a moderate meal: carbs + moderate protein
  • Examples: sandwich, rice with chicken, pasta
  • Still avoiding high fiber and very fatty foods

Before Squats

  • Stop heavy eating 60-90 minutes before first lift
  • Switch to light snacking if needed
  • Continue sipping fluids
  • Don't lift feeling stuffed or bloated

During the Meet

Between Lifts

You'll have significant time between squat, bench, and deadlift. Use this time wisely:

Good Between-Lift Foods

  • Bananas
  • Rice cakes with jam or honey
  • White bread with deli meat
  • Gummy candies (quick sugar)
  • Sports drinks
  • Small amounts of salty snacks

Avoid During the Meet

  • Heavy, fatty foods (burgers, pizza)
  • High fiber foods (beans, raw vegetables)
  • Dairy (can cause issues for some)
  • Large portions of anything
  • Anything you haven't eaten before training

Between Attempts

For the short breaks between individual attempts:

  • Sip water or sports drink
  • Quick sugar if energy is flagging (candy, sports gels)
  • Not a meal—just maintenance

Caffeine Strategy

Most lifters use caffeine. Plan your timing:

  • First dose before squats (30-60 min before)
  • Consider a second dose before deadlifts if it's a long meet
  • Use your normal dose—don't double up for "extra energy"
  • Be aware that caffeine + dehydration = not great
Watch the Caffeine

More is not better. If you normally use 200mg pre-workout, don't take 400mg because "it's a meet." You'll get jittery, anxious, and your heart rate will be all over the place. Stick to what works in training.

Hydration Throughout the Day

  • Sip fluids constantly—don't rely on thirst
  • Urine should be pale yellow
  • Mix of water and electrolyte drinks
  • Don't overhydrate to the point of discomfort

After the Meet

Once you're done lifting:

  • Continue rehydrating
  • Eat a proper meal with protein
  • Celebrate appropriately
  • Don't go too crazy—you'll feel it tomorrow if you do

Sample Meet Day Nutrition

Example Timeline (2-hour weigh-in, morning session)

  • 6:00 AM: Wake up, light breakfast if not cutting
  • 7:00 AM: Weigh-in
  • 7:00-7:30 AM: Start rehydration and eating
  • 8:00 AM: Moderate meal
  • 9:00 AM: Squats begin—light snacking only now
  • 10:00 AM: Done with squats—eat something
  • 11:00 AM: Bench—caffeine if needed
  • 12:00 PM: Lunch between bench and deadlift
  • 1:00 PM: Deadlifts—maybe more caffeine
  • 2:00 PM: Done—real meal

Common Mistakes

  • Overeating after weigh-in: You need fuel, not a feast. Bloating kills performance.
  • Undereating: The other extreme—running out of energy by deadlifts.
  • Trying new foods: GI issues on the platform are a nightmare.
  • Too much caffeine: Anxiety and jitters don't help lifting.
  • Forgetting to bring food: Venue food is often limited and unhealthy.
  • Dehydration: Especially after a cut—keep sipping.
The Bottom Line

Eat familiar foods, stay hydrated, don't overdo anything. Simple carbs for energy, moderate protein, low fiber and fat. Sip fluids throughout. The goal is sustained energy without GI distress. Nothing fancy—just consistent.
