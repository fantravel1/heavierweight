Knee Sleeves Guide

How to choose the right knee sleeves for training and competition.

10 min read Updated Dec 2024

Knee sleeves are one of the most common pieces of equipment you'll see in any strength sport. They keep your knees warm, provide proprioceptive feedback, and—depending on thickness—may add a small amount of support. Here's how to choose.

What Knee Sleeves Actually Do

  • Warmth: The neoprene retains heat, keeping joint fluid warm and reducing stiffness
  • Compression: Light compression improves blood flow and proprioception
  • Confidence: Many lifters feel more secure with sleeves on
  • Minimal support: Unlike wraps, sleeves provide very little mechanical advantage—maybe 5-10kg at most with very tight competition sleeves
Sleeves vs Wraps

Knee sleeves are allowed in "raw" or "classic" powerlifting divisions. Knee wraps are a separate category ("equipped" or "wraps" division) and provide significant mechanical assistance—potentially 20-50kg on your squat. They're different equipment for different purposes.

Thickness Options

Thickness Use Case Notes
3mm General training, warm-up Easy to put on, minimal support
5mm Training, CrossFit, general lifting Balance of comfort and warmth
7mm Powerlifting competition, heavy training Maximum allowed in IPF, more supportive

For powerlifting competition, 7mm is standard. For general training, 5mm is often more comfortable for high-rep work while still providing warmth.

Sizing: The Crucial Decision

Sizing is where most people go wrong. There are two approaches:

Training Fit

Easy to slide on and off, comfortable for extended wear. Size according to the brand's chart based on your knee measurement. Good for everyday training and high-rep work.

Competition Fit

Tight. Very tight. Often requires plastic bags, assistance, and struggle to get on. Provides maximum compression and support. Many competitors size down 1-2 sizes from their measured size for competition sleeves.

Two Pairs Strategy

Many serious lifters own two pairs: a looser training pair (true to size or slightly loose) and a tight competition pair (sized down). This saves wear on your comp sleeves and keeps training comfortable.

Top Recommendations

Best for Competition: SBD Knee Sleeves (7mm)

$90-100

The gold standard for IPF powerlifting. Extremely stiff when sized down, provides maximum legal support. Nearly every serious competitor uses these. They last for years. Worth the investment if you compete.

Best Value: Stoic Knee Sleeves (7mm)

$50-60

Similar stiffness to SBD at a lower price point. IPF approved. Great option if you want competition-level sleeves without the premium cost. Slightly less durable long-term.

Best for Training: Rehband 7mm

$70-80

The original. Softer neoprene than SBD, more comfortable for daily wear. Less supportive when sized down but excellent for warmth and general training. Trusted by athletes for decades.

Best Budget: Iron Bull Strength (7mm)

$30-40

Affordable entry point. Not approved for IPF competition but fine for training and most other federations. Good for beginners to see if they like training with sleeves before investing more.

Best 5mm Option: Rehband 5mm

$60-70

For those who find 7mm too thick. Great for high-rep training, CrossFit, or if you just want warmth without bulk. Easier to put on and more comfortable for extended wear.

How to Measure

Measure the circumference of your knee at the center of the kneecap, with your leg straight. Use a flexible tape measure.

  • For training fit: Use the size that matches your measurement
  • For competition fit: Go 1-2 sizes smaller than your measurement

When in doubt, check the specific brand's size chart—sizing varies between manufacturers.

Getting Them On

Tight sleeves are notoriously difficult to put on. Tips:

  • Plastic bags: Put a grocery bag over your foot/calf, slide the sleeve over it, then pull the bag out
  • Fold and roll: Fold the sleeve inside out, position it on your knee, then roll it up
  • Helper: Have a friend pull while you guide
  • Do it early: Put sleeves on during warm-ups, not right before your attempt

Care and Maintenance

  • Air dry after every use—don't leave them in your gym bag
  • Hand wash or gentle machine wash occasionally
  • Don't put in the dryer (degrades neoprene)
  • Store flat or hanging, not bunched up

With proper care, quality sleeves last 2-5 years of regular use.

Competition Rules

In IPF/USAPL:

  • Maximum 7mm thickness
  • Maximum 30cm length
  • Must be on the approved equipment list
  • Must be worn on the kneecap (not above or below)

Other federations have varying rules—always check before competition.

The Bottom Line

For competition, SBD 7mm sized down is the standard. For training, Rehband or a true-to-size 7mm provides warmth without the struggle. Don't overthink it—any quality 7mm sleeve will serve you well.
