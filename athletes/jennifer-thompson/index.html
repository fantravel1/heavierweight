Training Philosophy

Jennifer's approach to training emphasizes consistency over intensity. After more than two decades in the sport, she's learned that longevity comes from smart programming, proper recovery, and listening to her body.

"I don't train to failure regularly. I train to get better, and that means leaving something in the tank most sessions. The meets are where you go all out."

"Powerlifting isn't about being the strongest in the gym. It's about being the strongest on the platform when it counts. Everything else is just preparation."

Career Highlights

Jennifer began powerlifting in her early 20s and quickly rose through the ranks. Her first world title came in 2007, and she's dominated the 63kg class ever since. Advice for Aspiring Competitors

"Start with the basics and master them. Too many lifters want to jump to advanced techniques before they've built a foundation. Learn to squat, bench, and deadlift with solid form. The fancy stuff can come later."

"Find a coach or community. This sport is hard to do alone. Having people who understand what you're going through makes a huge difference‚Äîboth for technique feedback and mental support."
