Platform Commands

Referee signals and commands for squat, bench press, and deadlift.

10 min read Updated Dec 2024

In competition, you can't just lift when you want. The head referee controls the lift with verbal commands. Miss a command or move early, and the lift is red-lighted. Here's everything you need to know.

The Squat

The squat has two commands: one to start and one to rack. You unrack on your own, but everything else waits for the referee.

The squat has two commands: "Squat" to begin, "Rack" to finish
"SQUAT"
You may begin your descent
"RACK"
You may return the bar to the rack

The Bench Press

The bench has three commands: start, press, and rack. This is the lift with the most commands—and the most common source of red lights for new lifters.

Bench has three commands: "Start" (after bar is still on chest), "Press," and "Rack"
"START"
Bar is motionless on chest — ready to begin
"PRESS"
You may push the bar up
"RACK"
You may return the bar to the rack
The Most Important Rule

The bar must be COMPLETELY MOTIONLESS on your chest before the referee will give the "Press" command. Any sinking, bouncing, or movement resets the clock. Practice pause bench in training to make this automatic.

The Deadlift

The deadlift is the simplest: one command at the end. You start on your own, but must wait to be told to lower the bar.

The deadlift has one command: "Down" after you've locked out
"DOWN"
You may return the bar to the platform
Don't Drop the Bar

You must return the bar to the floor under control. Dropping the bar or letting go before it reaches the floor is a red light. Keep your hands on the bar until it's down.

Referee Lights

Three referees judge each lift: one head referee in front, two side referees. Each gives a white (good) or red (no good) light after the lift.

  • 3 white lights: Perfect lift, all judges agree
  • 2 white, 1 red: Good lift (majority rules)
  • 1 white, 2 red: No lift (majority rules)
  • 3 red lights: No lift, all judges agree
Source: Calgary Barbell - Understanding Competition Commands

Practice Tips

  • Train with pause bench: Every rep in training should have a pause
  • Practice commands: Have a training partner call commands
  • Wait longer than you think: In competition, the pause feels longer
  • Watch for "tell" habits: Don't bounce, sink, or move early
  • Attend a meet as a spectator first: See commands in action
