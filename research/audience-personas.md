## Primary Personas

### Persona 1: "Curious Casey" - The Beginner Explorer

**Demographics:**
- Age: 22-35
- Location: Urban/suburban US
- Education: College-educated
- Income: $40-80K

**Lifting Background:**
- 0-6 months experience
- May have done general fitness/CrossFit
- Interested in powerlifting or Olympic weightlifting
- No competition experience

**Goals:**
- Learn proper technique
- Build a consistent routine
- Understand programming basics
- Gain confidence in the gym

**Pain Points:**
- Gym intimidation
- Information overload online
- Fear of "getting bulky"
- Unsure how to start

**Content Needs:**
- Clear, non-intimidating beginner guides
- Program recommendations with rationale
- Equipment basics (what to buy first)
- Confidence and mindset content

**Content Consumption:**
- Mobile-first
- Prefers video and visual guides
- Saves articles for later reference
- Active on Instagram, TikTok

**Priority Pillars:** TRAIN (beginner), GEAR (basics), LIFE (confidence)

---

### Persona 2: "Progressing Priya" - The Serious Intermediate

**Demographics:**
- Age: 25-40
- Location: US, Canada, UK, Australia
- Education: College/graduate education
- Income: $50-100K

**Lifting Background:**
- 1-3 years experience
- Has run 1-2 structured programs
- Considering first competition or has 1-2 meets
- Understands basics, wants to optimize

**Goals:**
- Progress beyond beginner gains
- Prepare for competition
- Optimize training around life (work, family)
- Understand programming at deeper level

**Pain Points:**
- Plateau frustration
- Program hopping
- Balancing training with life demands
- Information paralysis on "optimal" approach

**Content Needs:**
- Intermediate programming deep-dives
- Competition preparation guides
- Federation comparisons
- Training optimization (cycle syncing, recovery)

**Content Consumption:**
- Mix of desktop and mobile
- Reads long-form content
- Subscribes to newsletters
- Active on Reddit (r/xxfitness, r/powerlifting)

**Priority Pillars:** TRAIN (intermediate), COMPETE, SCIENCE

---

### Persona 3: "Competing Carla" - The Active Competitor

**Demographics:**
- Age: 20-45
- Location: Global
- Education: Varied
- Income: Varied

**Lifting Background:**
- 3+ years experience
- 3+ competitions completed
- May have national-level aspirations
- Part of local lifting community

**Goals:**
- Qualify for higher-level competitions
- Optimize peaking and meet performance
- Stay informed on federation changes
- Connect with community

**Pain Points:**
- Finding quality coaching
- Managing weight classes
- Injury prevention/management
- Balancing competition with life

**Content Needs:**
- Advanced peaking protocols
- Federation rule updates
- Athlete profiles for inspiration
- Weight class strategy

**Content Consumption:**
- Engaged community member
- Follows athletes on social media
- Participates in forums/Discord
- Attends meets and events

**Priority Pillars:** COMPETE, TRAIN (advanced), GEAR (competition-specific)

---

### Persona 4: "Masters Melissa" - The 40+ Lifter

**Demographics:**
- Age: 40-60
- Location: US primarily
- Education: Established career
- Income: $80K+

**Lifting Background:**
- Varied: beginner to experienced
- May be returning to fitness
- Perimenopause or postmenopause
- Health-motivated as much as competition

**Goals:**
- Maintain/build strength through life changes
- Protect bone density
- Manage hormonal changes
- Compete in masters divisions

**Pain Points:**
- Hormonal changes affecting training
- Recovery taking longer
- Less representation in content
- Medical professionals unfamiliar with lifting

**Content Needs:**
- Masters-specific training guidance
- Menopause/perimenopause content
- Bone health and injury prevention
- "Starting later" inspiration

**Content Consumption:**
- Desktop preferred
- Values long-form, research-backed content
- Email newsletter subscriber
- Facebook groups

**Priority Pillars:** SCIENCE (menopause), TRAIN (masters), LIFE (health)

---

### Persona 5: "New Mom Natalie" - The Postpartum Lifter

**Demographics:**
- Age: 25-40
- Location: US, UK, Australia
- Education: College-educated
- Income: $60-120K (household)

**Lifting Background:**
- Experienced lifter pre-pregnancy
- Adjusting to postpartum body
- Eager to return to training
- May have pelvic floor considerations

**Goals:**
- Return to lifting safely
- Rebuild strength postpartum
- Manage pelvic floor issues
- Balance training with motherhood

**Pain Points:**
- Conflicting advice on postpartum exercise
- Pelvic floor dysfunction
- Time constraints
- Body image adjustments

**Content Needs:**
- Evidence-based postpartum return protocols
- Pelvic floor content
- Time-efficient training options
- Real stories from lifting moms

**Content Consumption:**
- Mobile (nursing, naptime browsing)
- Podcast listener
- Values quick, actionable content
- Mommy fitness communities

**Priority Pillars:** SCIENCE (postpartum), TRAIN (modified), LIFE (mom content)

## Secondary Personas

### Persona 6: "Plus-Size Penny"

**Needs:**
- Equipment that fits and works for larger bodies
- Technique modifications for different body types
- Representation without tokenization
- Weight class strategy for superheavyweights

**Content Focus:**
- Equipment sizing guides
- Superheavy athlete profiles
- Technique adjustments
- Body-neutral approach throughout

---

### Persona 7: "CrossFit-to-Powerlifting Carrie"

**Needs:**
- Transition guidance from CF to PL
- Understanding powerlifting programming
- Federation selection help
- Community connection

**Content Focus:**
- CrossFit to powerlifting content
- Program comparisons
- First meet preparation
- Federation guides

---

### Persona 8: "Olympic Olivia"

**Needs:**
- Weightlifting-specific content (vs. powerlifting)
- Finding coaching
- Understanding Olympic lifting federations
- Technique development

**Content Focus:**
- Olympic weightlifting beginner content
- USAW/IWF guides
- Snatch and clean & jerk technique
- Paris 2024 momentum content
