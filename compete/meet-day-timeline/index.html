Competition Day Timeline

A complete visual guide to pacing your meet day from weigh-ins to final deadlift.

Meet day can feel chaotic if you don't have a plan. This guide breaks down the entire day into manageable phases, with timing recommendations for warmups, nutrition, and mental prep.

The Big Picture: Meet Day Flow

Typical meet day is 4-6 hours from weigh-in to final deadlift

Warmup Timing Protocol

Timing your warmup is critical. Start too early and you'll be sitting around cold. Start too late and you'll rush through warmups or miss your opener.

Your last warmup should be ~5 lifters out from your opener—take it and head to the platform area

Sample Warmup Numbers

Fewer reps as weight increases—save energy for the platform

Key Warmup Rules

Take your last warmup at ~90% when you're about 5 lifters out. After that, stay warm and mobile but don't take more weight. Your opener IS your final warmup—it should feel like a warmup weight.

Nutrition Timing

Small, frequent feeds keep energy stable without causing GI distress

Mental Prep Between Lifts

Don't stay amped up for hours—cycle between relaxation and focus

Meet Day Bag Checklist

  • Singlet + approved t-shirt (if required)
  • Belt, wraps, sleeves, shoes
  • Backup of all equipment
  • Food: meals + snacks for full day
  • Water bottle + electrolytes
  • Caffeine source (pills, coffee, etc.)
  • Ammonia/smelling salts (if you use)
  • Chalk (verify if venue provides)
  • Phone charger
  • Warm layers for between lifts
  • Foam roller/lacrosse ball
  • Attempt card/notebook
Source: Calgary Barbell - Meet day walkthrough

Key Takeaways

  • Arrive early—rushing leads to mistakes and elevated stress
  • Time warmups by counting lifters, not the clock
  • Last warmup at ~90%, about 5 lifters out from your opener
  • Eat small, familiar foods throughout the day
  • Cycle between rest and focus—don't stay amped for 6 hours
  • Your opener should feel like a warmup—it IS your last warmup
