Changing the Game

Mattie Rogers did something unusual for an Olympic weightlifter: she became a genuine social media star. Her transparency about training, her personality, and her willingness to share both successes and failures helped bring a new audience to the sport.

But don't let the Instagram following fool you‚Äîshe's also an elite athlete. Her two Olympic appearances and multiple Pan American medals put her among the best American weightlifters of her generation.

"I want people to see that weightlifters are real people. We have bad days. We miss lifts. We deal with injuries and doubts. Showing that is part of the journey."

Olympic Journey

Mattie qualified for her first Olympics in Rio 2016, though the competition was marred by last-minute weight class changes due to doping disqualifications. She returned to the Games in Tokyo 2020, a testament to her longevity and consistency at the top level.

The road to Paris 2024 continues, with Mattie adapting her training and approach as she enters the later stages of her competitive career.

From CrossFit to Olympics

Like many American weightlifters, Mattie discovered the sport through CrossFit. Her natural talent was immediately evident, and she transitioned to dedicated weightlifting, quickly rising through the national ranks.

Training Philosophy

  • High-frequency training with technical focus
  • Extensive squatting and pulling volume
  • Regular mobility and recovery work
  • Mental preparation and visualization

Advice for Weightlifters

"Find joy in the process. The Olympics happen every four years, but training happens every day. If you can't enjoy the daily grind, you won't make it to the big stage."

"And don't be afraid to be yourself. The sport needs personality. It needs people who make it accessible. You can be serious about training and still have fun."
