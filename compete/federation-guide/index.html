Powerlifting Federation Guide

Understand your options and find the right federation for you.

12 min read Updated Dec 2024

The world of powerlifting federations can seem overwhelming. There are dozens of organizations, each with different rules, equipment standards, and drug testing policies. Here's what you need to know to choose the right one.

Why So Many Federations?

Unlike sports like weightlifting (one international federation), powerlifting has fragmented over decades due to disagreements about equipment, drug testing, and governance. This creates options—but also confusion.

The main divisions are:

  • Drug-tested vs. untested: Some federations have strict anti-doping; others don't test
  • Raw vs. equipped: Some focus on raw lifting; others allow supportive gear
  • Equipment standards: What brands of belts, sleeves, wraps are legal
  • Competition rules: Commands, depth standards, etc.

Major Federations (USA)

USAPL (USA Powerlifting)

The IPF affiliate in the United States. Drug-tested with WADA compliance. Raw-focused (classic) with some equipped divisions. Strictest equipment rules.

  • Drug testing: Yes (WADA/USADA)
  • Weigh-in: 2-hour
  • Equipment: IPF-approved list only
  • Path to Worlds: Yes (IPF)

USPA (United States Powerlifting Association)

Large federation with both tested and untested divisions. More flexible equipment rules. 24-hour weigh-ins. Drug Free and Non-Tested divisions.

  • Drug testing: Tested and untested divisions
  • Weigh-in: 24-hour
  • Equipment: Wider brand acceptance
  • Path to Worlds: Yes (IPL)

USPC (United States Powerlifting Coalition)

IPL affiliate, similar to USPA. Tested and untested. Good regional meet coverage in some areas.

  • Drug testing: Both divisions
  • Weigh-in: 24-hour
  • Equipment: IPL rules
  • Path to Worlds: Yes (IPL)

RPS (Revolution Powerlifting Syndicate)

Relaxed atmosphere, lifter-friendly. Tested and untested. Popular in the Northeast and expanding.

  • Drug testing: Both divisions
  • Weigh-in: 24-hour
  • Equipment: Flexible
  • Known for: Welcoming atmosphere

WRPF (World Raw Powerlifting Federation)

Growing federation. Untested (Pro) and tested (Amateur) divisions. Flexible equipment. Strong social media presence.

  • Drug testing: Tested and untested
  • Weigh-in: 24-hour
  • Equipment: Flexible
  • Known for: Big meets, strong community

Key Considerations

Drug Testing

If competing drug-free is important to you, choose a tested federation. USAPL has the strictest testing (WADA/USADA protocols). Other tested divisions exist but may have less rigorous testing programs.

If testing isn't a concern, untested federations often have more meets, 24-hour weigh-ins, and a more relaxed atmosphere.

Weigh-In Type

  • 2-hour weigh-in (USAPL): Weigh in 2 hours before lifting. Limits aggressive water cuts but allows moderate manipulation.
  • 24-hour weigh-in (USPA, RPS, etc.): Weigh in the day before competing. Allows more aggressive cuts followed by full rehydration.

For first-timers, 24-hour weigh-ins are more forgiving if you're close to a weight class cutoff.

Equipment Rules

USAPL/IPF has an approved equipment list—specific brands of belts, sleeves, singlets, and shoes are legal. You must check the list before purchasing.

Other federations are more flexible. Most quality equipment from reputable brands will be allowed.

First Meet Tip

For your first meet, choose based on what's available locally and what fits your schedule. Don't stress about finding the "perfect" federation. Get experience first; you can switch later if your goals evolve.

Competition Standards

Rules vary slightly between federations:

  • Squat depth: Hip crease below top of knee. Fairly consistent, though judging strictness varies.
  • Bench press: Pause on chest, commands vary slightly (some use "start," others "press")
  • Deadlift: Lockout standards, down command (some require, some don't)

Watch videos from meets in your chosen federation to understand their specific standards.

Raw vs. Equipped

Most newer lifters compete "raw" (belt, knee sleeves, wrist wraps only). Equipped lifting uses supportive gear:

  • Raw/Classic: Belt, knee sleeves (up to 7mm), wrist wraps
  • Single-ply: Single layer supportive suits/shirts
  • Multi-ply: Multiple layers of supportive gear (maximum support)

Most federations offer raw divisions. Equipped lifting is a different sport with different technique and requires significant learning.

Finding Meets

  • OpenPowerlifting.org: Database of past meets and lifter histories
  • Federation websites: Meet calendars for each organization
  • LiftingCast: Meet registration platform used by many federations
  • Local gyms: Often know about nearby meets

Membership and Costs

Most federations require annual membership ($50-100) plus meet entry fees ($50-150 typically). Some allow single-meet memberships.

USAPL memberships can only be used for USAPL meets. Some other federation memberships (like USPA) may have reciprocity agreements.

Path to Higher Competition

If your goal is international competition:

  • IPF World Championships: Through USAPL → qualify at nationals
  • IPL World Championships: Through USPA/USPC → qualify at nationals

For most recreational competitors, this isn't an immediate concern. Focus on getting experience and setting PRs.

Choosing Your Federation

Choose USAPL if...

  • Drug-free competition is essential to you
  • You want the strictest, most standardized judging
  • You aspire to IPF Worlds someday
  • You prefer 2-hour weigh-ins

Choose USPA/RPS/WRPF if...

  • You want 24-hour weigh-ins
  • You want more flexible equipment rules
  • There are more meets available in your area
  • You prefer a more relaxed meet atmosphere
The Bottom Line

Your first meet should be whatever's accessible and fits your schedule. Federation choice matters more as you develop competitive goals. Start somewhere, get experience, and adjust based on what you learn about yourself as a competitor.
