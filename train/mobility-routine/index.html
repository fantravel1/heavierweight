Mobility Routine for Lifters

Targeted stretches to improve your squat, bench, and deadlift positions.

15 min read Updated Dec 2024

Mobility isn't about being able to do the splits—it's about having the range of motion to hit proper positions in your lifts. This routine targets the key areas that limit most lifters: hips, ankles, thoracic spine, and shoulders.

When to Do Mobility Work

  • Before training: Dynamic stretches and movement prep (see our warm-up guide)
  • After training: Static stretches when muscles are warm
  • Off days: Dedicated mobility sessions (15-20 minutes)
Static vs Dynamic

Static stretching (holding positions) is best after training or on off days. Before lifting, use dynamic stretches that take joints through range of motion with movement. This routine is designed for post-training or dedicated mobility days.

Hip Mobility

Tight hips limit squat depth, sumo deadlift width, and overall lower body positioning. These stretches target hip flexors, adductors, and external rotators.

90/90 Hip Stretch 60-90 sec/side
Targets: Hip internal & external rotation

How to: Sit with front leg bent 90° in front, back leg bent 90° behind. Keep torso upright. Lean forward over front leg for deeper stretch. Switch sides.

Couch Stretch 60-90 sec/side
Targets: Hip flexors, quads

How to: Place back foot against wall, shin vertical. Step front foot forward into a lunge. Squeeze the glute on the back leg to intensify hip flexor stretch.

Pigeon Pose 60-90 sec/side
Targets: Glutes, hip external rotators (piriformis)
Source: Squat University - Hip Mobility Routine

Ankle Mobility

Limited ankle dorsiflexion causes forward lean in squats and heel rise. This is one of the most common squat limiters.

Wall Ankle Stretch 30-60 sec/side
Targets: Ankle dorsiflexion, calf

Thoracic Spine Mobility

A stiff upper back limits squat positions (can't stay upright) and bench setup (can't retract shoulders fully). These drills open up the thoracic spine.

Foam Roller Thoracic Extensions 10-15 reps
Targets: Thoracic extension
Cat-Cow 10-15 reps
Targets: Spinal flexion/extension, segmental mobility
Source: Squat University - Thoracic Spine Mobility

Shoulder Mobility

Shoulder mobility matters for squat bar position and bench press setup. Limited external rotation and extension restricts these positions.

Doorway Pec Stretch 30-60 sec/side
Targets: Pectorals, anterior shoulder

Sample Mobility Routine

This 15-minute routine hits all the key areas. Do it after training or on rest days.

Exercise Duration Target
Cat-Cow 10 reps Spine warm-up
Foam Roller T-Spine 10-15 reps Thoracic extension
90/90 Hip Stretch 60 sec/side Hip rotation
Couch Stretch 60 sec/side Hip flexors
Pigeon Pose 60 sec/side Glutes/piriformis
Wall Ankle Stretch 45 sec/side Ankle dorsiflexion
Doorway Pec Stretch 45 sec/side Chest/shoulders
Consistency Over Intensity

10-15 minutes of mobility work 4-5 days per week beats one long session. Mobility changes take weeks to develop. Make it a habit, not an event.
