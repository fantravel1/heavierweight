Breathing and Bracing

Master the Valsalva maneuver and 360-degree bracing for maximum core stability and spinal protection.

Proper bracing is the foundation of heavy lifting. It creates intra-abdominal pressure (IAP) that stabilizes your spine, allowing you to transfer force efficiently and lift heavier weights safely. Without it, you're lifting with a wet noodle for a core.

Understanding Intra-Abdominal Pressure

Think of your torso as a cylinder. When you brace correctly, you're pressurizing that cylinder from the inside—pushing out equally in all directions against your abdominal wall and belt (if wearing one).

Push out equally in all directions—front, sides, and back. Not just the front!

The Bracing Sequence

1

Stand Tall

Neutral spine, ribs down over pelvis

2

Big Breath

Fill your belly like a balloon, not your chest

3

Expand 360°

Push out against your belt and sides

4

Lock Down

Close the glottis, hold pressure throughout lift

Breathe into your belly and sides, not your chest. Your shoulders should NOT rise.

Breathing Timing by Lift

Squat

One breath per rep—don't breathe during the lift itself

Bench Press

Maintain arch while breathing—don't let chest collapse when you exhale

Deadlift

Brace before you hinge down—don't try to breathe while bent over the bar
Source: Juggernaut Training Systems - Complete bracing tutorial

Belt Synergy

A belt doesn't replace bracing—it enhances it. The belt gives your expanding midsection something to push against, increasing intra-abdominal pressure even further.

The belt gives your brace something to push against, amplifying IAP

How Tight?

Tight enough that you can get one finger between the belt and your stomach when relaxed, but it feels snug when you brace into it. Too loose = no feedback. Too tight = can't expand properly.

Key Takeaways

  • Breathe into your belly and sides, not your chest—360° expansion
  • One breath per rep—hold through the entire movement
  • Brace BEFORE you move—setup breath for squat/deadlift, post-unrack for bench
  • Belt amplifies your brace but doesn't replace proper technique
  • Practice bracing with light weight until it becomes automatic
