Clean & Jerk Technique Guide

Master the most technical lift in the gym with step-by-step instruction.

15 min read Updated Dec 2024

The clean and jerk is one of two competition lifts in Olympic weightlifting. It combines explosive pulling, a deep front squat catch, and an overhead press or split. Here's how the movement works.

The Clean & Jerk Phases

The clean & jerk sequence: Setup → First Pull → Extension → Catch → Stand → Dip → Drive → Split Jerk

The Clean

The clean brings the bar from the floor to the front rack position (on your shoulders). It consists of:

1. Setup

Feet hip-width, bar over midfoot, hook grip, shoulders over or slightly in front of bar. Back flat, chest up, lats engaged.

2. First Pull

Push the floor away. Keep chest and hips rising together. Bar stays close to shins. Controlled, not explosive yet.

3. Second Pull (Extension)

This is where the power happens. Violently extend hips, knees, and ankles. Bar makes contact at upper thigh/hip. Triple extension launches the bar upward.

4. Third Pull / Turnover

Pull yourself UNDER the bar. Elbows whip around fast. Drop into a front squat position with the bar on your shoulders.

5. Catch & Stand

Receive the bar in a deep front squat. Elbows high, torso upright. Stand up to complete the clean.

The Jerk

The jerk takes the bar from shoulders to overhead. Most lifters use the split jerk.

1. Setup

Bar resting on shoulders, elbows slightly in front. Feet hip-width. Take a breath, brace core.

2. Dip

Bend knees 4-6 inches, keeping torso vertical. Dip must be straight down—no forward lean. Load the legs like a spring.

3. Drive

Explosively drive up through the legs. This launches the bar off your shoulders. Arms stay relaxed until...

4. Split & Lockout

Split your feet—front foot forward, back foot back—and punch arms to lockout simultaneously. You're catching the bar overhead with legs split.

5. Recovery

Bring front foot back, then back foot forward to stand with feet together under the locked-out bar.

The split jerk: vertical dip, explosive drive, split under with locked arms.

Common Errors

  • Pulling with arms too early: Arms stay long until after extension. Let the hips do the work.
  • Not getting under the bar: You must actively pull yourself down, not just lift the bar up.
  • Dip going forward: Jerk dip must be straight down. Forward lean kills power transfer.
  • Pressing out the jerk: The bar should lock out instantly from leg drive, not press slowly.
  • Slow elbows in the clean: Fast elbows = successful catch. Slow elbows = crash.
Get Coaching

The Olympic lifts are highly technical. While this guide provides fundamentals, working with a qualified weightlifting coach—even for a few sessions—will accelerate your learning and help you avoid ingrained bad habits.

Learning Progressions

Don't try to learn the full lift day one. Common progressions:

  1. Clean: Clean pull → Hang clean (above knee) → Hang clean (below knee) → Full clean
  2. Jerk: Push press → Push jerk → Split jerk with no dip → Full split jerk
