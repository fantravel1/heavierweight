Contribute to HEAVIERWEIGHT

Help us build the best resource for women in strength sports. We're looking for experienced lifters, coaches, and researchers to share their knowledge.

HEAVIERWEIGHT is built on community expertise. Whether you're a competitive athlete, a coach, a researcher, or someone with deep experience in a specific area, we want to hear from you.

What We're Looking For

We publish content across six pillars. Here's what we need:

TRAIN

Programming guides, technique breakdowns, exercise tutorials, periodization strategies

COMPETE

Meet prep, federation guides, competition strategy, athlete interviews

SCIENCE

Research summaries, female physiology, hormone considerations, biomechanics

GEAR

Equipment reviews, buying guides, comparison articles, home gym builds

FUEL

Nutrition protocols, supplement guides, recovery strategies, weight management

LIFE

Mindset, community building, gym culture, balancing lifting with life

Who Should Apply

  • Competitive athletes with experience to share
  • Coaches working with female lifters
  • Researchers in exercise science, nutrition, or related fields
  • Writers with lifting experience and strong communication skills
  • Specialists in areas like physical therapy, sports psychology, or sports medicine
We Value Lived Experience

You don't need a PhD to write for us. If you've competed, coached, or lived the lifting life, your perspective matters. We especially want to hear from women in strength sports.

Content Standards

Our content is:

  • Evidence-based: Claims backed by research or substantial experience
  • Practical: Actionable advice readers can implement
  • Female-focused: Written for and about women in strength sports
  • Honest: No hype, no broscience, no conflicts of interest
  • Accessible: Clear writing that respects the reader's intelligence

The Process

1

Pitch

Send us a brief pitch (100-200 words) describing your article idea and why you're qualified to write it.

2

Approval

We'll review your pitch and respond within 7 days. If approved, we'll discuss scope, length, and timeline.

3

Draft

Write your article according to our style guide. First-time contributors typically write 1,500-2,500 words.

4

Edit

Our editorial team provides feedback. Most articles go through 1-2 revision rounds.

5

Publish

Your article goes live with full byline, bio, and links to your social/coaching presence.

Compensation

We pay for published articles. Rates depend on length, complexity, and your experience level. First articles typically range from $75-200. Established contributors can negotiate higher rates.

Beyond payment, contributors get:

  • Full byline and author bio
  • Links to your coaching, social media, or personal site
  • Cross-promotion on our social channels
  • Access to our contributor community

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I write about something not on your content list?

Absolutely. If it's relevant to women in strength sports, we want to hear about it. Pitch it.

Do I need to be a woman to contribute?

No, but your content must be focused on and relevant to women lifters. We prioritize but don't require female contributors.

What if I've never written professionally?

Experience matters more than credentials. If you can explain complex topics clearly, you're qualified. We provide editorial support.

Can I republish content I've written for HEAVIERWEIGHT?

After 90 days, you can republish on your own platforms with a link back to the original. We retain first publication rights.
