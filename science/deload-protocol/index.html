Deload Protocol

Strategic recovery weeks that dissipate fatigue and set up your next phase of progress.

A deload is a planned reduction in training stress that allows accumulated fatigue to dissipate while maintaining the fitness you've built. Skip deloads and you'll plateau or get injured. Time them right and you'll supercompensate—coming back stronger than before.

Why Deloads Work

Fatigue masks fitness. Deloads let fatigue drop, revealing your true strength.

Signs You Need a Deload

2-3 signs from any category = time to deload. Don't wait for injury.

Deload Methods

Volume Reduction

Keep intensity (weight) the same, cut sets by 40-60%. Maintains strength qualities while reducing fatigue.

Example: 5x5 → 2x5 at same weight

Intensity Reduction

Keep volume (sets/reps) similar, reduce weight by 10-20%. Good for joint recovery.

Example: 5x5 @ 80% → 5x5 @ 65%

Both Reduced

Cut both volume and intensity. Most aggressive recovery approach.

Example: 5x5 @ 80% → 3x5 @ 65%

Active Rest

No barbell work. Light conditioning, mobility, bodyweight only. For severe fatigue or pre-competition.

Example: Walks, stretching, swimming
Volume deload maintains intensity feel; intensity deload gives joints a break

Deload Timing

More advanced = more fatigue accumulated = more frequent deloads needed
Source: Renaissance Periodization - Science of deloading

Common Deload Mistakes

Going too hard: A deload should feel easy. If you're grinding reps, you're not deloading. Skipping completely: Zero training can backfire—light movement keeps patterns sharp. Adding extra work: Don't fill the "extra time" with conditioning or accessory volume. Rest is the point.

Key Takeaways

  • Deloads dissipate fatigue, revealing strength you've already built
  • Schedule proactively every 3-5 weeks, don't wait for breakdown
  • Reduce volume OR intensity by 40-60%, not necessarily both
  • Light movement beats complete rest for most lifters
  • Advanced lifters need more frequent deloads than beginners
  • If you feel amazing during deload week, you probably needed it more than you thought
