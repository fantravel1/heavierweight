The Deadlift Specialist

Jessica Buettner is known for one thing above all: her deadlift. Watching her pull is something special‚Äîthe bar bends, hangs for a moment, and then moves with surprising speed. Her 250kg pull put her among the best deadlifters in the world, regardless of weight class.

But Jessica is more than just a strong deadlift. She's a complete lifter who has worked hard to bring her squat and bench up to match, making her a genuine contender in international competition.

"The deadlift just clicks for me. I love the simplicity of it‚Äîthe bar is on the ground, and your job is to pick it up. There's something pure about that."

Competing with Type 1 Diabetes

Jessica was diagnosed with Type 1 diabetes as a child. Rather than let it limit her, she's become one of the sport's most visible advocates for athletes with diabetes.

What is Type 1 Diabetes?

Type 1 diabetes is an autoimmune condition where the body doesn't produce insulin. It requires constant management‚Äîmonitoring blood sugar, administering insulin, and adjusting for activity. Jessica does all this while competing at the highest level of powerlifting.

She's open about the challenges: managing blood sugar during long competition days, timing meals around lifting, and dealing with the unpredictability of the condition. Her message is clear: diabetes doesn't have to stop you from pursuing elite athletics.

"I want other people with diabetes to see that this is possible. It's harder, yeah. You have to plan more, adjust more, think more. But it's absolutely possible to be strong and compete at a high level."
