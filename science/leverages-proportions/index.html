Leverages and Body Proportions

How your unique anatomy determines your optimal technique in each lift.

No two lifters are built exactly alike. Limb lengths, torso proportions, and hip structure all influence what technique works best for YOU. Understanding your leverages helps you stop fighting your anatomy and start working with it.

Key Proportions That Matter

Three ratios have the biggest impact on technique selection:

Your proportions are fixed—optimize technique around them, not against them

Squat: Femur Length Effects

Femur length relative to torso is the biggest determinant of squat style. Long femurs require more forward lean to keep the bar over midfoot.

Long femurs aren't a disadvantage—they just require a different technique approach

Bench: Arm Length and Grip

Arm length affects both ROM and optimal grip width. Longer arms mean more distance to travel but can use a wider grip.

Short arms = bench advantage; long arms = deadlift advantage. Everyone has a "best" lift.

Deadlift: Arm + Torso Interplay

Deadlift leverages are the most complex—arm length, torso length, and hip structure all interact to determine whether conventional or sumo suits you better.

These are tendencies, not rules—some lifters break the mold. Test both stances.
Source: Calgary Barbell - Finding your optimal technique

How to Measure Your Proportions

Femur : Torso Ratio

Stand sideways, mark hip crease and knee center. Compare femur length to seated torso height. >1.0 = long femur relative to torso.

Arm Span : Height

Measure fingertip to fingertip with arms out. Compare to standing height. >1.0 = "long" arms (like Michael Phelps). Good for deadlift.

Hip Structure

Lie on back, pull knee to chest, then out to side. If you can get thigh parallel to floor easily, you likely have "good" hip structure for sumo.

The Key Insight

You can't change your proportions, but you CAN optimize technique around them. Stop trying to squat like someone with different leverages—find what works for YOUR body. The best lifters understand their own anatomy.

Key Takeaways

  • Long femurs → low bar, wider stance, more forward lean squat
  • Short arms → bench advantage, may need compensations on deadlift
  • Long arms → deadlift advantage, max grip width on bench
  • Short torso + long arms = conventional deadlift tendency
  • Long torso + good hips = sumo deadlift tendency
  • Proportions explain why different lifters look different—embrace your structure
