Accessory Exercise Selection Guide

Build a balanced accessory program that addresses your weak points and supports your main lifts.

Accessory exercises are the support system for your competition lifts. Choosing the right accessories means identifying weak points and selecting movements that directly address them while building overall strength and muscle.

Understanding Weak Point Analysis

Before selecting accessories, you need to identify where your lifts break down. This diagram shows common sticking points and what they indicate:

Accessory Categories

Accessories fall into three tiers based on their carryover to competition lifts:

Prioritize higher-tier accessories early in training, add lower tiers as hypertrophy phases approach

Squat Accessories

For Weak Quads (Mid-Range Sticking Point)

Front Squat

Forces upright torso, maximizes quad demand. 3x6-8.

Targets: Quads, upper back

Leg Press

High quad volume without spinal loading. 3x10-15.

Targets: Quads, glutes

Belt Squat

Quad-focused with zero back fatigue. 3x8-12.

Targets: Quads, adductors

Pin Squat (Mid)

Builds strength at exact sticking point. 4x3-5.

Targets: Quads at weak ROM

For Weak Glutes/Hips (Out of Hole)

Pause Squat

Eliminates stretch reflex, builds bottom strength. 3x4-6.

Targets: Glutes, quads

Hip Thrust

Direct glute isolation at full hip extension. 3x10-12.

Targets: Glutes

Good Morning

Builds posterior chain and hip hinge pattern. 3x8-10.

Targets: Glutes, hamstrings, back

Box Squat

Teaches explosive hip drive from dead stop. 4x3-5.

Targets: Glutes, hips
Choose accessories based on your specific weak points, not just random exercises

Bench Press Accessories

For Weak Off-Chest (Pecs/Shoulders)

Spoto Press

Pause 1 inch off chest, builds bottom strength. 3x5-6.

Targets: Pecs, front delts

Dumbbell Press

Greater ROM and pec stretch. 3x8-12.

Targets: Pecs

Incline Press

Emphasizes upper pec and front delt. 3x8-10.

Targets: Upper pecs, shoulders

Dips

Builds pressing power through full ROM. 3x8-12.

Targets: Pecs, triceps

For Weak Lockout (Triceps)

Close Grip Bench

Direct tricep emphasis in bench pattern. 3x6-8.

Targets: Triceps

Board Press / Pin Press

Overloads lockout portion. 4x3-5.

Targets: Triceps at lockout

JM Press

Hybrid skull crusher/close grip. 3x8-10.

Targets: Triceps

Tricep Pushdown

High volume tricep isolation. 3x12-15.

Targets: Triceps

Deadlift Accessories

For Weak Off Floor (Legs/Position)

Deficit Deadlift

Increases ROM, builds floor strength. 3x4-6.

Targets: Quads, positioning

Pause Deadlift

Pause at knee, reinforces position. 3x4-5.

Targets: Quads, back tightness

Front Squat

Builds quad strength for initial drive. 3x6-8.

Targets: Quads

For Weak At/Above Knee (Back/Glutes)

Block Pull

Overloads top portion, builds confidence. 4x3-5.

Targets: Back, glutes

Romanian Deadlift

Builds hamstring/glute through hinge. 3x8-10.

Targets: Hamstrings, glutes

Barbell Row

Builds back strength for maintaining position. 3x8-10.

Targets: Lats, upper back

Good Morning

Posterior chain builder for hip extension. 3x8-10.

Targets: Hamstrings, glutes, back

Programming Guidelines

Start with 3-5 accessories per session. Main lift fatigue accumulates—you don't need endless volume. Prioritize quality reps over exercise variety. Rotate accessories every 4-8 weeks to prevent staleness.

Key Takeaways

  • Identify sticking points through video analysis before selecting accessories
  • Prioritize Tier 1 (variations) during strength blocks, add more Tier 3 during hypertrophy
  • Match accessory ROM to where you're weak—not just what's popular
  • 3-6 accessories per session is sufficient for most lifters
  • Rotate exercises every 4-8 weeks to continue progressing
