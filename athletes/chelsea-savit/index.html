The Deadlift Machine

Chelsea Savit has built a reputation as one of the most impressive deadlifters pound-for-pound in women's powerlifting. Pulling over 4x bodyweight puts her in rarefied air‚Äînumbers that would be impressive at any weight class, let alone at 52kg.

Her sumo deadlift is technically refined to the point of artistry. Every rep looks the same: wide stance, explosive off the floor, smooth lockout. There's no grind, no struggle‚Äîjust efficient movement that makes heavy weight look easy.

"The deadlift is honest. Either you can pick it up or you can't. There's no hiding, no cheating. That's what I love about it."

Technical Breakdown

What makes Chelsea's deadlift so effective:

  • Wide sumo stance: Optimal for her leverages
  • Patient setup: Takes time to get perfect position
  • Hip wedge: Gets hips close to the bar before pulling
  • Leg drive: Initiates with legs, not back
  • Straight bar path: No deviation, pure vertical

Watching her lift is educational. Every position serves a purpose. Nothing is wasted.

Building the Total

While the deadlift gets the headlines, Chelsea has worked relentlessly to build her squat and bench to match. A 1000+ lb total at 52kg is extraordinary, and it requires all three lifts performing.

Her approach: dominate the deadlift while steadily improving the other lifts. The deadlift carries meets, but the total wins them.

Training Philosophy

  • Technical perfection over maximum effort
  • High frequency pulling
  • Sumo-specific accessory work
  • Competition simulation regularly
  • Patience with the process
"You don't build a big deadlift overnight. You build it over years of consistent, smart training. There are no shortcuts‚Äîjust the work."

Advice for Developing Deadlifters

"Find your stance and own it. Whether you pull conventional or sumo, commit to it and become the best at that style. Don't constantly switch chasing what seems easier."

"And work your weaknesses. If you're slow off the floor, train that. If you struggle at lockout, train that. The deadlift shows you exactly where you need to improve‚Äîlisten to it."

Competition Mindset

Chelsea approaches meets with methodical precision. Openers are conservative to build momentum. Second attempts put her in good position. Thirds are where records happen.

"Never miss your opener. That's rule one. Put a total on the board, then hunt PRs. Getting greedy early is how you bomb out."

  • Socks: Tall deadlift socks
