Best Powerlifting Belts for Women

How to choose the right belt for squats, deadlifts, and competition.

15 min read Updated Dec 2024

A good belt is one of the best investments you can make for your lifting. It helps you brace more effectively, can add pounds to your squat and deadlift, and when chosen right, will last for decades.

This guide covers everything you need to know: what a belt actually does, how to choose between lever and prong, why 10mm might be better for you than 13mm, and our specific recommendations.

What a Belt Actually Does

A belt doesn't support your spine directly. Instead, it gives your abdominal muscles something to push against, increasing intra-abdominal pressure. This makes your entire trunk more rigid and stable.

Think of it like this: you create pressure by bracing hard. The belt amplifies that pressure by preventing your stomach from expanding outward. More pressure = more stability = more strength.

When to Use a Belt

Use a belt for heavy compound movements (squats, deadlifts, overhead press). Most lifters belt up at around 70-80% of max and above. There's no need to wear it for everything.

Lever vs. Prong

Feature Lever Prong
Speed Faster on/off Slower
Adjustability Fixed position (needs screwdriver to change) Multiple holes
Weight fluctuation Harder to adjust Easy to adjust
Durability Lever can break (rare) Very durable
Price Usually more expensive Usually cheaper

Recommendation

Lever if: You compete (speed matters between attempts), your weight is stable, you prefer convenience.

Prong if: Your weight fluctuates, you're on a budget, you want something simpler.

10mm vs. 13mm

Thickness matters more than you might think, especially for women with shorter torsos.

10mm

  • More comfortable for most women
  • Breaks in faster
  • Better for deadlifts (less digging in at bottom)
  • Works well for shorter torsos
  • Sufficient support for most lifters

13mm

  • Maximum stiffness and support
  • Takes longer to break in
  • Can dig into ribs/hips
  • Better for very heavy squats
  • May be overkill for many lifters
Our Take

Most women should start with 10mm. It provides excellent support while being more comfortable. If you're squatting 2x+ bodyweight and want maximum rigidity, then consider 13mm.

Sizing for Women

Belt sizing is based on your waist measurement at the navel—where you'll actually wear the belt. Common mistakes:

  • Don't use pants size. That's measured lower.
  • Measure relaxed AND braced. Your waist expands when you brace.
  • When in doubt, size up. You can always tighten; you can't add holes.

Width Considerations

Standard powerlifting belts are 4 inches (10cm) wide—the maximum allowed in competition. Some women with shorter torsos find this digs into their ribs or hips. Options:

  • 3-inch belts (still competition legal in most feds)
  • Tapered belts (narrower in front, wider in back)
  • Adjusting belt position (slightly higher or lower)

Breaking In Your Belt

New leather belts are stiff. Here's how to break them in:

  1. Roll the belt up tightly and leave it overnight
  2. Bend it back and forth along the length
  3. Wear it for training sessions (it'll be uncomfortable at first)
  4. Some people apply leather conditioner; others don't bother

A 10mm belt typically takes 2-4 weeks to break in. A 13mm belt can take 1-2 months. Be patient.

Competition Considerations

If you plan to compete, check your federation's approved equipment list:

  • IPF/USAPL: Strict approved list (SBD, Inzer, Titan, etc.)
  • USPA: More flexible, most major brands allowed
  • Other feds: Vary widely, check before buying
The Bottom Line

A 10mm lever or prong belt from a reputable brand (Inzer, SBD, Pioneer) will serve you well for years. Don't overthink it—pick one that fits your budget and federation requirements, and start using it.
