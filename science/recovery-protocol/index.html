Science

Recovery Protocol

Optimize recovery between sessions with evidence-based strategies prioritized by impact.

You don't get stronger in the gym—you get stronger recovering FROM the gym. Training creates the stimulus; recovery creates the adaptation. Most lifters overtrain and under-recover. Here's how to flip that script.

The Recovery Hierarchy

Not all recovery modalities are equal. Focus on the fundamentals before chasing marginal gains from expensive gadgets.

Fix the base of the pyramid before adding modalities at the top

Tier 1: The Non-Negotiables

Sleep: Your #1 Recovery Tool

Sleep is when growth hormone peaks, muscle protein synthesis is highest, and your nervous system recovers. No amount of supplements or massage replaces poor sleep.

The pre-sleep routine matters as much as time in bed

Nutrition for Recovery

You can't recover from what you can't fuel

Tier 2: Force Multipliers

Stress Management

Training IS stress. Life stress adds to the total load. Your body can't tell the difference between work deadlines and heavy squats—both deplete your recovery capacity.

All stress is cumulative—when life is hard, train smarter not harder

Tier 3: The Extras

These are the "1% gains" that only matter once the fundamentals are locked in:

Foam Rolling

Temporary relief, doesn't speed recovery. Good for pre-training mobility.

Massage

Feels good, limited evidence for faster recovery. Mental benefit is real.

Ice Baths

May blunt hypertrophy. Use sparingly, maybe pre-comp only.

Compression

Minimal effect. Doesn't hurt, probably doesn't help much either.

Supplements

Creatine works. Most others are marginal at best. Food first.

Sauna

Good for relaxation and heat acclimation. Recovery benefit unclear.

The Hard Truth

Most "recovery modalities" are marketing. If you're sleeping 5 hours and eating poorly, no amount of NormaTec boots will save you. Fix the basics first—they're free and proven.

Recovery Between Training Days

Balance hard training with genuine rest—intensity requires recovery

Key Takeaways

  • Sleep is 80% of recovery—prioritize 7-9 hours of quality sleep
  • Protein intake (1.6-2.2g/kg) and adequate calories fuel adaptation
  • All stress is cumulative—reduce life stress or reduce training volume
  • Active recovery means LIGHT activity—walks, mobility, not extra training
  • Most recovery gadgets are marketing—focus on free fundamentals first
  • Consistency beats intensity—sustainable training beats heroic sessions
